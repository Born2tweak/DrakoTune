# H — Professional Listening Recruitment Report

**As of:** 2026-07-21  
**Sources:** S-P01–S-P04.

## Finding

Professional evidence should begin with a paid 2–3 engineer pilot, not a large arbitrary-`n` study. SoundBetter and AirGigs can identify working professionals; AES sections and education networks can support trained/expert outreach; Prolific can recruit broader panels and publishes a current minimum of $8/hour and recommended $12/hour. Qualified engineers warrant a professional premium based on task time, preparation, and rights requested.

## Qualification

Record verifiable role, years/credits, recent target-genre work, monitoring environment, hearing/accessibility disclosures appropriate to the task, conflicts, and familiarity with the tested material. Do not equate a marketplace profile with study consent or reuse permission.

## Rights and ethics

Consent must separately cover response retention, anonymized analysis, audio access, quotations, public result publication, benchmark inclusion, training, future contact, and withdrawal. Commissioned processing needs separate grants for the contributed audio/treatment. Compensation cannot depend on response direction or passing a preference endpoint.

## Pilot deliverable

Estimate task duration, failure/attrition, variance components, tie/no-difference frequency, device compliance, unclear language, and plausible effect sizes. Use those estimates for a simulation-based confirmatory design. Recruitment and spending remain owner-approved actions.

| S-E09 simulation-based power | Pilot variance/effect/attrition can drive final design simulation. | Justifies small protocol pilot before confirmatory allocation. | Two or three engineers cannot support an efficacy claim or stable subgroup inference. | Label pilot exploratory; freeze confirmatory plan at DT-60. |

## Qualification and independence record

For every expert, store evidence of role, recent target-genre credits, years and task relevance, monitoring environment, conflicts, familiarity with source material, prior exposure to DrakoTune output, and which panel estimand they enter. General listeners and professional engineers remain separate panels unless the preregistration estimates their interaction.
