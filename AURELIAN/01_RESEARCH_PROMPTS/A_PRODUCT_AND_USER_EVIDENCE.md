# A — Product and User Evidence

**Decision:** define the first evidence-backed product promise and target workflow.

Investigate current independent-vocal cleanup/mixing workflows for rap/pop creators and engineers. Separate isolated-vocal cleanup, in-mix processing, creative style transfer, and mastering-adjacent tasks. Seek primary product documentation, reproducible workflow evidence, and direct user research; do not infer demand from model papers. Record user role, input context, desired control, failure cost, privacy expectation, and acceptable wait time.

**Exclusions:** broad market-size claims, DAW/plugin expansion, or competitor feature copying without user evidence.

**Deliverable:** prioritized job map, unsupported assumptions, launch strata, and experiments needed.

**Stop when:** each proposed promise has an observable outcome, target user, evidence strength, and disconfirming test.
