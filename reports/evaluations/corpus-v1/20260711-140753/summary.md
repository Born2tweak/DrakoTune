## Findings (M23 — objective evidence only)

**Reading the numbers correctly:** SI-SDR punishes *any* waveform reshaping, not
just failed repair. A compressor or EQ move that listeners might prefer still
lowers SI-SDR because the output deviates from the clean reference. Negative
deltas therefore mean "the chain reshapes more than it restores toward the
reference," NOT "the output sounds worse." Perceptual verdicts belong to the
M24 listening test (ADR 0004). Within that frame:

1. **v2 dominates on gain staging:** low_level family ΔSI-SDR ≈ −19..−22 dB
   (v2) vs ≈ −47..−56 dB (generic/legacy). v2's gain objective restores level;
   the remaining negative delta is the usual compressor/EQ reshaping.
2. **v2 ≥ legacy in 20 of 21 cells** on SI-SDR. Legacy's extra moves (notably
   its air boost) push outputs further from the reference; on harshness,
   legacy shows a *positive* defect-band delta (+0.009..+0.010) — it makes the
   harsh band relatively hotter. Objective legacy failure mode, recorded for M26.
3. **Hum is the clearest capability gap:** mild/moderate hum is essentially
   untreated (only the 100 Hz-max HPF clips it at strong severity, v2 +2.9 dB).
   No notch processor exists. This is the objective justification for the M28
   hum-notch candidate.
4. **Generic "wins" many cells by doing less** — the fixed chain changes the
   waveform least. This is the well-known fidelity-metric bias toward inaction
   and is exactly why preference must be measured by humans, loudness-matched.
5. **In-song noise is not removable by a gate:** noise family deltas hover near
   zero at best; the gate only helps pauses. Matches the validation plan's
   claims-prohibited list (no broadband denoise claim).
6. **Safety gates all green:** output clipping ratio 0.0000 in every cell;
   zero loudness-match refusals among 480 engine runs.

M28 gate evidence — hum notch objectively justified; M24 — perceptual verdict
